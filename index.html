Cities in Crisis

Photo Manipulation

Cities in Crisis is a series of photo-manipulated images that aims to draw attention to the looming yet often overlooked issue of urban pollution. Representing surface water pollution is the city of Dhaka, Bangladesh- the skyline of which I have turned into a cascading spring of stained liquid. Next is the city of Shanghai, a bustling city concealed under a ghostly veneer of smog and haze. Last but not least- light pollution is embodied by the cityscape of New York’s Manhattan. While the city radiates laser-like brilliance, the surrounding electrical sparks reflects the intense power consumption behind the city’s glowing façade.

By materializing the cities of Dhaka, Shanghai and New York in their respective element of pollution; I have re-envisioned the cities to embody the very essence of harm they emit.

APA Book Award Intro

Animation

The APA hosts an annual Book Award for books published by an Australian publisher or designed by Australian designers. Following in the footsteps of 2012 PANZ Book Design Awards in New Zealand, the APA wanted an animated introductory sequence that celebrates the books whilst engaging and delighting the audience.

My animated intro expounds the theme of discovery whereby the discovery of adventures in reading is often only made possible beautiful, clever and meaningful book cover designs.

Performative Perfumery

Identity

Performative Perfumery is a DIY perfume blending kit that allows people to mix scents according to personality traits and qualities they wish to project on to others.

Exploring the notion of ‘otherness’ within the self, this speculative design project highlights the critical importance of conscious and mindful performance in the process of self-invention.
